MY MUSIC STORY


About Me My Music Story


Playing the piano is one of my favourite things to do. It really brings the artistic side of me, taking away all negative feelings and stress.

I was introduced to music when I was 3 years old, starting with Little Notes classes from Yamaha in Singapore, where I learnt to dance, sing and appreciate music. I still remember having a whale of a time playing music games, singing ‘Five Little Chickadees’, and playing around with the triangle, castanets and the tambourine!

I started learning the piano at the age of 8. From then on, my adventure with music began. Whenever I mastered a piece, I would feel exhilarated. Looking at the audiences’ eyes after performing, I knew I had successfully transferred the emotion of the piece to them.

At the age of 10, I completed ABRSM Grade 2 with Distinction. In a year's time, I also managed to complete ABRSM Grade 4 with Distinction. After moving to Wellington for a year, I have achieved Distinction in my Trinity Grade 5 examination too.

My Examination Pieces



Sonatina in E Flat Major - Georg Anton Benda (1722-1795)

(Grade 6 Trinity Piano Exam Piece 2018-2020)

This piece is one of my examination pieces. It is a lovely piece, with a nice contrast between the different melodies. However, I find it the hardest out of all of my examination pieces, due to the fact that there are parts where I have to move my hands quickly, which is a problem since I recorded this at the beginning of winter in New Zealand, so my hands were cold.



In Dreams - Howard Shore/Fran Walsh

(Grade 6 Trinity Piano Exam Piece 2018-2020)

This is by far my favourite piece out of all three of my examination pieces, because, it has a very simple, yet meaningful melody, followed by the beautiful flow of notes at the end of the piece. I find that nobody should have any trouble with this piece. It is easy, yet amazing.



Gymnopedie no.3 - Erik Satie

(Grade 6 Trinity Piano Exam Piece 2018-2020)

The Gymnopedie has a very unique melody on the right hand, with even more unique chords on the left hand. What I love about this piece is that it is very slow and simple. The harder part is that being the fact that it is slow, it will be harder to fascinate the audience.


My Composed Pieces



Spring Dew

This piece is my first ever composed piece. I composed this in 2018, when I was just became 11 years old. I loved this piece because it is very calm. The transition from D major to A major and back to D major was very smooth and subtle (or so I think). The reason I named this piece "Spring Dew" was because the song feels like a fresh and new beginning - just like from winter to spring.



Blue Waves

This piece is my second composed piece. I also composed this in 2018 and I liked this piece too. The bass notes that I play with my right hand repeat occasionally, giving a slight sense of warmth and secureness. I really liked the ending because of that reason too. I named this piece "Blue Waves" because the left hand feels like waves, and because it is very calming, hence the colour blue.


My Favourite Pieces



Prelude - J.S.Bach

I really like this piece because it is very harmonic and the change of keys flows really well. It also does not sound 'old', despite being composed in the 17th Century.
